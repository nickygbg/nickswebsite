1.6 Describing access issues that need taking into account - describe issues that people with disabilities may face when using web pages and how we can overcome them.

There are many disabilities to consider catering for on a website. These are the main ones below.

Text to speech

This is for people who are blind. The computer can speak to you and tell you what commands are available for the user to press.

Colour aids for the visually impaired.

Certain people can only see a certain aspect of the colour spectrum. A site needs to make sure that there are different colour options for the situation.

Dyslexic assistant.

Sites can make sure that they do not use a white background this can assist a person with dyslexia.

Epilepsy protection

When creating a website, do not add any flashing images or videos that can cause people with epilepsy to get an attack.
